Pressure Testing
(Pipeline / Tank / Vessel)

* Hydro testing
* Air testing
* Nitrogen testing
* Oil testing

Marine pressure testing ensures pipelines, tanks, and vessels can safely withstand operating pressures. It includes hydro testing (using water to detect leaks and structural integrity), air testing (compressed air for low-risk systems), nitrogen testing (inert gas for high-safety environments), and oil testing (using oil for systems sensitive to water exposure).

Hydro Jetting / Retro Jetting
(Up To 30000 Psi)

* Pipeline / Main Condenser / Cooler tubes & Boiler tubes.
* Chemical Cleaning and Hot Oil Flushing for Marine / Petrochemical Industries
*Chemical Cleaning - Condenser / All Types of Heat Exchanger
* Chemical Cleaning - All Types of Boilers.

Hydro jetting and retro jetting up to 30,000 psi provide powerful cleaning for pipelines, main condensers, cooler and boiler tubes. These methods remove scale, debris, and fouling. Chemical cleaning supports marine and petrochemical industries, effectively restoring condensers, heat exchangers, and all types of boilers to improve efficiency and equipment performance.

Repair Work in Anchorage Offshore

Repair work in anchorage offshore refers to maintenance and corrective activities carried out on a vessel while it is anchored away from the shore, usually in designated safe anchorage zones. These repairs are performed when the ship cannot enter port immediately, or when the required work is minor yet essential for safe operations. Typical offshore repairs include machinery troubleshooting, hull inspections, pipeline or valve replacement, pump servicing, electrical fault rectification, and emergency temporary repairs.
Performing repairs at anchorage helps reduce downtime, avoid costly port delays, and ensure the vessel remains compliant with safety and operational standards. Skilled marine engineers and technicians travel by launch boat to the vessel and carry out the necessary work using portable tools and equipment. Strict safety protocols, communication procedures, and environmental guidelines are followed to ensure smooth operations. Offshore anchorage repairs play a vital role in maintaining vessel reliability and minimizing operational disruption.

Hydro Jet Cleaning & Painting

Hydro jet cleaning and painting is a specialized surface-preparation and coating process widely used in marine, industrial, and offshore environments. Hydro jet cleaning involves using high-pressure water—often ranging from 3,000 to 30,000 psi—to remove rust, scale, old paint, marine growth, oil deposits, and other contaminants from metal surfaces. This method is highly effective because it cleans deep into pores without damaging the metal or creating dust, making it safer and more environmentally friendly than traditional abrasive blasting.
Once the surface is thoroughly cleaned and achieves the required cleanliness grade, painting work begins. High-performance marine or industrial coatings are applied to protect the cleaned surface from corrosion, chemical exposure, and harsh environmental conditions. Proper painting improves asset lifespan, ensures structural integrity, and enhances overall appearance. Combined, hydro jet cleaning and painting provide a durable, long-lasting solution for ships, tanks, pipelines, and offshore structures, ensuring reliable protection and efficient maintenance.

Rope access welding, fabrication, cleaning, painting, and NDT

Rope access welding, fabrication, cleaning, painting, and NDT for ships refers to performing essential repair and maintenance tasks at difficult-to-reach areas of a vessel using certified rope access technicians. Instead of scaffolding or heavy equipment, technicians use specialized ropes and harnesses to safely access vertical or confined spaces such as ship sides, cargo holds, funnels, tanks, and offshore structures.
Through rope access, welding and fabrication work can be carried out to restore damaged steel, install new components, or reinforce structural areas. Cleaning and painting tasks involve removing rust, scaling, and contaminants, followed by applying protective coatings to prevent corrosion. Non-Destructive Testing (NDT), such as ultrasonic testing, magnetic particle testing, and dye penetrant testing, is also performed to assess metal thickness, detect cracks, and ensure structural integrity.
This method is fast, cost-effective, and minimizes vessel downtime while maintaining high safety standards and reliable quality.

Engine Mechanical Work

Ship engine mechanical work involves the maintenance, repair, and overhauling of a vessel’s main engine and auxiliary machinery to ensure smooth, safe, and efficient operation. Marine engines operate under heavy load and harsh conditions, so regular mechanical work is essential to prevent breakdowns and maintain performance.
Typical tasks include inspecting and servicing components such as pistons, cylinder liners, fuel injectors, turbochargers, pumps, compressors, and cooling systems. Engineers check engine performance parameters, adjust fuel timing, clean filters, replace worn-out parts, and troubleshoot mechanical faults. Overhauling may involve dismantling major engine components, measuring clearances, calibrating systems, and reassembling to manufacturer standards.
Mechanical work also covers maintenance of auxiliary engines, generators, air systems, steering gear, and propulsion components. All tasks follow strict safety procedures and guidelines to ensure reliability. Proper engine mechanical work extends the engine’s life, improves fuel efficiency, reduces downtime, and ensures the vessel remains seaworthy and compliant with regulations.
